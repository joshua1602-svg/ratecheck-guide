Valuation Methodology

Comparable Evidence

Comparable hereditaments have been identified and analysed in accordance with established rating valuation principles, having regard to location, use (VOA Special Category), and physical characteristics. Primary emphasis is placed on geographic proximity to the subject property, reflecting the principle that rental tone is most reliably evidenced by nearby properties. {% if business_type == 'retail' or business_type == 'hair_beauty' %} For retail hereditaments, greatest weight is given to properties located on the same parade or street where a consistent body of evidence exists, before extending to the wider postcode sector and surrounding locality where required. {% else %} The geographic scope is expanded as necessary to ensure a sufficient and representative body of evidence, reflecting typical market density for the property type. {% endif %} Comparables are restricted to those within a relevant range of net internal area (NIA), and the dataset is reviewed to exclude anomalous observations, ensuring that the analysis reflects the prevailing tone of the list rather than isolated outliers.

Assessment of Tone and Rateable Value

The assessment of rateable value is derived from analysis of comparable rental tones, expressed as £/m², having regard to the tone of the list. In determining the appropriate tone, reliance is placed on the central body of consistent evidence, with a weighted approach applied to reflect the relative evidential strength of each comparable. Weighting reflects proximity, alignment of floor area, and quality of evidence, with greater reliance placed on directly evidenced VOA assessments. The adopted tone is applied to the subject property's net internal area to form an opinion of rateable value.

Physical Characteristics and Layout

Consideration is given to the physical configuration of the subject property and its comparability to available evidence. The subject property is assessed in terms of the proportion of trading space relative to ancillary/storage accommodation, together with overall floor configuration, including the presence of basement or upper floor areas. Where comparable properties include equivalent VOA survey detail, their internal configuration has been reviewed against that of the subject property, with greater reliance placed on hereditaments demonstrating a higher degree of functional and structural similarity. {% if business_type == 'restaurant_cafe' %} For restaurant and café hereditaments, specific regard is given to the positioning of the kitchen, with ground floor kitchen arrangements typically providing stronger comparability due to their impact on trading efficiency and value. {% endif %}

Data Source and Valuation Basis

This assessment is informed by analysis of the Valuation Office Agency’s published rating list and bulk data underpinning the 2026 Rating List, including rateable values and associated property attributes relied upon in the compilation of assessments. The valuation is undertaken by reference to 1 April 2024, being the statutory antecedent valuation date applicable to the 2026 Rating List. All conclusions therefore reflect market conditions at that date, in accordance with the statutory hypothesis and established principles of rating valuation.

Assumptions and Limitations

This analysis is based on publicly available VOA data and information provided by the ratepayer. No inspection of the subject property or the comparable hereditaments has been undertaken, and reliance has been placed on the accuracy and completeness of the available records. While a structured and evidence-based methodology has been applied, the assessment remains dependent on the quality and scope of underlying data. Matters not apparent from published records — including property condition, specific layout nuances, or recent physical or occupational changes — have not been taken into account unless expressly stated. This report is provided for the purpose of supporting a potential Check, Challenge, Appeal submission and represents an analytical opinion of rateable value having regard to the available evidence. It does not constitute a formal Red Book valuation, nor professional advice from a Chartered Surveyor or regulated valuer. No responsibility is accepted for decisions taken solely on the basis of this report without further professional advice or independent verification.
